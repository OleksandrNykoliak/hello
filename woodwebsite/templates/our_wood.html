Properties of glued panels

Oak glued panels made from natural wood have different specifications of length, width and thickness. It is made by splicing planed all round timbers between each other. Depending on the characteristics of the timbers (length, width), different types of seams are formed in the panels: parallel with the joint along the length (massive panel) or finger-joint (timbers connected along the length and width). This is a strong, durable material, widely used in carpentry and engineering.​

Technical characteristics of wood

  • Density – 675 kg/m³
  • Elastic modulus (GPa) – 10.6
  • Tensile strength (MPa) – 97.1
  • Compressive strength (MPa) – 46.3
  • Hardness (Brinell coefficient) – 3.7
  • Radial shrinkage – 4.7%
  • Tangential shrinkage – 8.4%
  • Volume shrinkage – 13.0%

Use of glued panels

Wood glued panel is universal in application. This is a popular material for such works:

  • Furniture manufacturing: countertops, beds, cabinet furniture, children’s and office furniture, chairs, armchairs, sofas.
  • Interior design: wall panels, decorative elements, hinged shelves.
  • Stairs
  • Doors, windows and other carpentry.
